\DiaryEntry{Complex Analysis, 2}{2026-02-05}{Complex Analysis}

We reconsider the complex exponential,

\bee
f(z) = e^{z} = e^x (\cos y + j \sin y)
\eee

Splitting this into the real- and imaginary function, we obtain

\bee
u(x,y) = e^x \cos y + j e^x \sin y
\eee

We can insert these into the Cauchy-Riemann equations to check whether the function is analytic,

\bee
\frac{\partial u}{\partial x} = \frac{\partial v}{\partial y}, \quad \frac{\partial u}{\partial y} = - \frac{\partial v}{\partial x}
\eee

For the complex exponential function we have

\begin{align*}
    \frac{\partial u}{\partial x} &= e^x \cos y \\
    \frac{\partial v}{\partial y} &= e^x \cos y \\
    \frac{\partial u}{\partial y} &= - e^x \sin y \\ 
    \frac{\partial v}{\partial x} &= e^x \sin y
\end{align*}

We see that the Cauchy-Riemann equations and conclude that the complex exponential is an analytic function. We can now obtain the complex derivative as

\bee
f'(z) = \frac{\partial u}{\partial x} + \frac{\partial v}{\partial x} = e^x \cos y + j e^x \sin y = e^z \qed
\eee
